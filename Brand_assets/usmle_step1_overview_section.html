Proven Scientific Method to Study USMLE

Students are spending countless hours studying and not seeing results. Research from three leading medical institutions tells us exactly why — and what actually works.

2011
Albert Einstein College of Medicine

"Are questions the answer?" — The analysis found that the number of study hours alone didn't predict better scores. Students who completed ~2,000 practice questions saw significantly improved results. It's about questions, not just hours.

2014–2015
University of Michigan

"Study behaviors and USMLE Step 1 Performance" — Students who completed three or more passes through First Aid and combined it with unique and repeat practice questions were significantly more successful on the exam.

2015
Washington University of St. Louis

"Student-directed retrieval" — Practice questions are strongly associated with Step 1 performance. Anki use showed significant correlation with scores. The study also noted that depression and test anxiety were negative predictors — wellness matters.
